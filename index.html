Project Idea

Overview

The aim of this project is to create a smartphone app for RMIT students which helps them to find live study space and other facilities. Students will be able to open the app and select what they are looking for, for example a study space or a microwave, the app will then present them with the available buildings which have those amenities and provide directions to the location. The app updates in real time whether there are study spaces so that students don’t waste their time having to go and check several buildings to study.

Motivation

RMIT has seen a constant increase in enrolments over the years and in 2019 there were ‘97,000 student enrolments’ (RMIT Statistics at a Glance 2019, p.2). With on campus learning returning in 2021 there will be thousands of new and returning students who may find it difficult to navigate the campus for the first time or may have even forgotten where things are located as RMIT was online for almost the entirety of 2020. During my first year of university, I remember finding it difficult to find my classes and other facilities which resulted in me being late a handful of times and often frustrating. Therefore, the idea of building an application which would help other students not face the issue as me seemed like a good project.

Description

Once students have downloaded the iOS or android version of the app, they will be presented with a home page with a list of facilities the app can help them to find. Facilities such as study spaces, microwaves, toilets, help desks, libraries and entrances and exits to building as these have changed due to covid restrictions. Once a facility has been selected the app will show the nearest available location and provide directions of necessary.

There have been plenty of times where I have had to search through multiple levels and building just to find a place to study. Hence one of the key features of this app will be the live study space finder. All study spaces will be loaded on to the app so when a student is at a particular building and level, they will have the ability to mark out on the app if there are more spots available at that particular space or if it is full. From these reports the app will automatically get updated and let students know whether a particular study space is available, or it is full.

Another feature which will be implemented is that students can enter the building, level, and room number for their timetabled classes. The app will then save these and provide directions to all the classes which is particularly important for first and second-year students as this will be their first time on campus due to mainly all of last year being online. Similar to the live study space finder students can report any other useful information such as if a particular entrance is closed, pop up events and hazards etc.

Tools and Technologies

As the app needs to be available for students with android and iOS devices code will need to be written in java and Swift. Hence software such as Android studio and XCode will need to be installed. In terms of hardware to develop the apps a MacBook will be necessary to install XCode and the android version can also be developed on macOS. As we are developing a smartphone app no other equipment is necessary for this project.

Skills Required

Programming knowledge of Java and Swift are necessary to write the code for both the android and iOS version of the apps. Other technical skills include being able to develop a good user interface which is aesthetic, consistent with other apps the users may have used, ease of use and error prevention (Nielsen Norman Group 1994). With their being various resources and tools which are designed to help people design apps it is very feasible to obtain the skills to carry out this project. The hardware requirements are also easily obtained as all that is necessary is a device which has macOS like a MacBook for example so that the iOS version of the app can be developed.

Outcome

If this project is successful it will make the lives of students easier by helping them to find the facilities they are looking for quickly which saves them the trouble of having to ask around and waste time. This development aims to build a culture of students helping other students by reporting if study spaces are available or full along with any other useful information they would like to report.
